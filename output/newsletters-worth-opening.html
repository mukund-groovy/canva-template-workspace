Subject Line First

Your subject line is a promise. Is it curious, useful, or urgent?

Write it before the email, keep it under nine words, and never overpromise what the body cannot deliver.

Nobody remembers five things at once. Pick a single message, build the whole email around it, and cut everything that competes. A focused email is a finished email your reader can act on.

Formal copy feels like a form. Read it aloud.

If you would not say it to a friend across a table, rewrite it. Short sentences, real words, and a clear voice keep people reading.
