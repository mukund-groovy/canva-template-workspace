Delete The Line That Says

Hope You're Well

Your opening line is throat-clearing. Just circling back, quick question, per my last email — none of it earns the read. Start on the sentence you were building up to. Pleasantries belong at the bottom, where nobody has to wade through them.

03 / 07

One Email Should Carry

Exactly One Ask

Three requests in one message reliably return zero answers. People reply to the ask they can finish in a minute, then quietly park the rest forever. Pick the one that actually matters, give it its own line, and send the others tomorrow.

The Subject Line Is A

Filing Decision

It is not a title, it is a triage label. Budget sign-off, needs you Thursday tells a busy reader what it is, what it wants and when it expires — all from the notification. Vague subjects get opened last, which is the slow way of saying never.

05 / 07

Say It The Way You

Would Out Loud

Nobody has ever said please find attached herewith to another human being. Office language is a costume we put on to sound careful, and it lands as distance. Write it the way you would say it across a desk: plain words, short sentences, your own voice.

A Long Email Is Not The

Same As Respect

Every extra paragraph you add to sound courteous is work you are quietly handing to somebody else, and they feel the weight of it. Four lines, one ask, done. Brevity is the kindest thing you can send a person whose day is already full.
