How To Keep Your Sourdough Starter

Alive & Bubbly

Five easy habits that turn flour, water and a little patience into a starter you can actually trust.

1

Feed It On A Rhythm

Pick a feeding time and stick close to it. A starter fed at roughly the same hour each day learns the pattern, rises faster, and gets a lot less confusing to read.

LOAFLY
02 / 07
2

Skip The Fridge At First

For the first two weeks, keep your starter on the counter, not in the fridge. Room temperature keeps the yeast active while it is still building up its strength.

LOAFLY
03 / 07
3

Watch, Don’t Just Wait

Forget the clock. Look for a dome on top, bubbles through the sides, and a jiggle when you nudge the jar — that tells you far more than any timer will.

LOAFLY
04 / 07
4

Save The Discard

The starter you scoop out before feeding is not trash. Stir it into pancakes, crackers or flatbread so nothing good ever goes down the drain.

LOAFLY
05 / 07
5

Trust A Good Sour Smell

Sharp and tangy is healthy — that is just the yeast doing its job. Fuzzy spots or streaks of pink or orange are the only real reason to start over.

LOAFLY
06 / 07

Keep It Rising

Every loaf starts with a jar you kept alive one small feeding at a time. Save this guide, and let your starter do the rest.
