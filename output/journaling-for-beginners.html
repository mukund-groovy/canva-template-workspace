Step one

Pick One Honest Line

Skip the perfect opening. Write the truest sentence you can manage right now — what happened, what you noticed, or what you're avoiding — and let that one honest line pull the next one out of you.

Step two

Set A Five-Minute Timer

A blank page feels like homework, so keep the bar low. Open the notebook, start a five-minute timer, and write until it rings. Momentum comes from repetition, never from one perfect session.

03

Step three

Leave The Notebook In Sight

A journal buried in a drawer never gets opened. Keep it on the nightstand, the desk, or the bag, so reaching for it takes zero willpower — the habit sticks once the friction disappears.
