Open With Story Not Stats

Numbers convince the mind, but a story earns the room. Start with a real moment — a customer, a failure, a spark — before a single chart appears.

Name The Problem First

Nobody funds a solution to a problem they don't feel. Spend your first thirty seconds making the pain specific and real before you mention your product.

Keep It Painfully Simple

If a smart stranger can't repeat your idea back in one sentence, it isn't ready to pitch. Cut every slide that needs a footnote — clarity beats cleverness.

Show Proof Not Promises

Traction, however small, outweighs any forecast. One paying customer or one working prototype says more than a five-year revenue projection ever will.
