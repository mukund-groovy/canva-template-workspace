8 Fresh Ways To

Bake With Citrus

Bright, juicy bakes for citrus season, one tip at a time.

01

Zest Before You Juice

Grate the peel first — every bit of zest you save now is flavor you can’t get back once the fruit is already juiced.

ZESTNOTE
02 / 10
02

Mix Your Citrus

A blend of orange, lemon, and lime keeps a bake from tasting one-note, letting the sharp fruit balance the sweet.

ZESTNOTE
03 / 10
03

Salt Wakes The Sweet

A small pinch in the batter makes citrus taste brighter, not salty — it’s there to sharpen, not season.

ZESTNOTE
04 / 10
04

Warm The Juice First

Cold juice straight from the fridge can seize melted butter, so let it sit out while you prep everything else.

ZESTNOTE
05 / 10
05

Candy The Peel

Don’t toss the rind — simmered in sugar syrup, it turns into a chewy, glossy topping worth saving for last.

ZESTNOTE
06 / 10
06

Taste The Fruit First

Citrus sweetness swings by the week, so taste a slice before you commit to the sugar in the recipe.

ZESTNOTE
07 / 10
07

Glaze While Warm

A citrus glaze soaks into warm cake far better than cold, so brush it on the moment the bake leaves the oven.

ZESTNOTE
08 / 10
08

Store Away From Light

Whole citrus keeps its oils longest in a cool, dark spot — sunlight fades the flavor before the fruit even softens.

ZESTNOTE
09 / 10
Final Thought

The best citrus bake starts with fruit you actually tasted first.
