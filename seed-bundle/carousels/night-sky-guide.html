5 Steps To Your First Night Under The Stars

A short field guide to finding true darkness, reading the sky, and staying out past the streetlights.

Step 01

Find Real Darkness

Drive twenty minutes past the last streetlight. A dark-sky map shows the nearest low light-pollution zone — even one class darker reveals hundreds more stars.

Starlog
02
Step 02

Let Your Eyes Adjust

Give your eyes twenty minutes without white light. Switch your phone to red mode and resist checking it — night vision resets the moment you glance at a screen.

Starlog
03
Step 03

Learn Three Anchor Stars

Start with Polaris, Orion's Belt, and the Big Dipper. Once you can find these three, every other constellation is just a hop away.

Starlog
04
Step 04

Chase The New Moon

Check the lunar calendar before you go. A new moon means the darkest sky of the month — a full moon can wash out everything but the brightest planets.

Starlog
05
Step 05

Pack Light, Stay Late

Bring a red flashlight, a blanket, and a star chart app. Then put the phone away — the best views come after the first hour of patience.
