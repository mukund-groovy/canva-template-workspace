How to start

Journaling

A gentle guide to the daily writing habit — in eight small steps.

Start Here

Pick Your Notebook

Step 1

Choose a journal that feels good to hold — lined, dotted, or blank. The one you love is the one you'll actually use.

Next Slide

Set A Time

Step 2

Anchor it to a habit you already have. With your morning coffee or last thing at night — same time, every day.

Next Slide

Start Small

Step 3

Three lines is enough. Don't aim for pages — aim for showing up. Momentum will always beat length.

Next Slide

Write Freely

Step 4

Skip the perfect sentence. Spelling, grammar, tidiness — none of it matters here. Just let the words flow.

Next Slide

Use Prompts

Step 5

Stuck on a blank page? Ask one question. What went well today? What's on my mind? Then let it lead.

Next Slide

Make It Yours

Step 6

Add washi tape, doodles, pressed flowers, or photos. A journal you decorate is one you'll keep coming back to.

Next Slide

Keep It Private

Step 7

This page is only for you. No audience, no edits, no judgement. Honesty grows best in a safe, quiet space.

Next Slide

Look Back Often

Step 8

Reread old entries now and then. You'll see how far you've come — and spot patterns you'd have missed.
